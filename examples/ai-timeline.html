Early Foundations
1950

The Turing Test

Alan Turing proposes the "Turing Test" as a measure of machine intelligence. His paper "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" asks the fundamental question: "Can machines think?"

Impact: Established the philosophical foundation for AI research and created the first benchmark for machine intelligence.
1956

Birth of AI

The Dartmouth Summer Research Project coins the term "Artificial Intelligence." John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, and others gather to explore machine intelligence.

Impact: Officially launched AI as a field of study and attracted the first major research funding.
1966

ELIZA Chatbot

Joseph Weizenbaum creates ELIZA, the first chatbot that could engage in simple conversations by pattern matching and substitution.

Impact: Demonstrated that simple algorithms could create convincing human-like interactions, sparking interest in conversational AI.
1980s

Expert Systems Boom

Expert systems like MYCIN (medical diagnosis) and XCON (computer configuration) demonstrate AI's commercial potential in specialized domains.

Impact: First successful commercial AI applications, generating billions in revenue and proving AI's business value.
Strategic AI
1997

Deep Blue Defeats Kasparov

IBM's Deep Blue becomes the first computer to defeat a world chess champion in a match, beating Garry Kasparov 3.5-2.5.

Impact: Proved AI could exceed human expertise in complex strategic thinking, capturing global attention.
Deep Learning Era
2006

Deep Learning Renaissance

Geoffrey Hinton's breakthroughs in deep neural networks spark the modern AI revolution. GPU computing makes training large neural networks feasible.

Impact: Launched the current era of AI, enabling breakthroughs in computer vision, speech, and language understanding.
2012

ImageNet Breakthrough

AlexNet wins the ImageNet competition with a 15.3% error rate, dramatically outperforming traditional computer vision approaches (26.2% error).

Impact: Proved deep learning's superiority in computer vision, triggering massive investment in AI research.
2016

AlphaGo Masters Go

DeepMind's AlphaGo defeats world champion Lee Sedol 4-1 in the ancient game of Go, previously thought impossible for computers due to its complexity.

Impact: Showed AI could handle intuitive, creative thinking previously exclusive to humans.
Generative AI Boom
2020

GPT-3 Language Revolution

OpenAI releases GPT-3 with 175 billion parameters, demonstrating unprecedented natural language understanding and generation capabilities.

Impact: Sparked the generative AI boom, leading to ChatGPT and the current AI adoption wave across industries.
2022

ChatGPT Goes Mainstream

OpenAI launches ChatGPT, reaching 100 million users in just 2 months—the fastest growing consumer application in history.

Impact: Made AI accessible to everyone, transforming how people work, learn, and create content globally.

The Next Frontier

As we look toward 2025 and beyond, AI continues evolving toward artificial general intelligence (AGI), promising to revolutionize every aspect of human society—from healthcare and education to scientific discovery and creative expression.
