The History of AI

Seven decades of breakthroughs — from a simple thought experiment to agents that think, code, and create.

🧪 1950

The Turing Test

Alan Turing publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," proposing the imitation game — the first formal framework for evaluating machine intelligence.

🎓 1956

Dartmouth Workshop

John McCarthy coins "Artificial Intelligence" at a Dartmouth summer conference. The field is officially born, attracting early pioneers like Minsky and Simon.

🏥 1970s

Expert Systems

Rule-based programs like MYCIN and DENDRAL bring AI into real-world domains — medical diagnosis, chemical analysis, and business decision-making.

❄️ 1987

The AI Winter

Hype collapses as expert systems fail to scale. Funding dries up, DARPA pulls back, and the Lisp machine market crashes — AI enters its deepest winter.

♟️ 1997

Deep Blue Defeats Kasparov

IBM's Deep Blue becomes the first computer to beat a reigning world chess champion in a full match, captivating the world and reigniting interest in AI.

🖼️ 2012

ImageNet & Deep Learning

AlexNet crushes the ImageNet competition, slashing error rates by 10+ points. Deep learning goes from niche to mainstream, launching the modern AI era.

🎮 2016

AlphaGo Defeats Lee Sedol

DeepMind's AlphaGo beats the world Go champion 4-1, a feat thought decades away. Move 37 in Game 2 becomes one of the most famous moves in history.

📝 2020

GPT-3 & Few-Shot Learning

OpenAI's 175-billion-parameter model stuns researchers with emergent abilities — writing code, essays, and poetry from just a few examples.

💬 2022

ChatGPT Goes Viral

ChatGPT reaches 100 million users in two months — the fastest-growing consumer app ever. AI goes from research curiosity to dinner-table conversation.

🧠 2023

GPT-4 & Claude

Multimodal models arrive — GPT-4 passes the bar exam, Claude pioneers constitutional AI. The race for safe, capable frontier models intensifies.

🤖 2024–25

The Age of Agents

AI breaks out of the chat box. Autonomous agents write code, browse the web, manage workflows, and collaborate — ushering in a new era of AI-human partnership.
