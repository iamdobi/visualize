A CAROUSEL GUIDE

7 Habits of Highly Effective Engineers

Swipe through to level up your engineering career →

1 / 8
01

Write It Down

Document decisions, architecture, and context. Your future self and teammates will thank you endlessly.

02

Ship Small, Ship Often

Small PRs get reviewed faster, break less, and build momentum. Aim for daily deploys.

03

Read the Error Message

Seriously. 80% of debugging is reading what the computer already told you. Slow down and read.

04

Automate the Boring Stuff

If you've done it three times, script it. Invest 30 minutes now to save hours later.

05

Ask Why, Not Just How

Understanding the business context behind a task helps you build the right thing, not just build the thing right.

06

Review Others' Code

Code review is the fastest way to learn patterns, catch bugs early, and build shared ownership across the team.

07

Protect Your Focus

Block deep-work time. Turn off Slack. A 4-hour uninterrupted block is worth more than a full day of context-switching.
