Pros & Cons

Remote Work

Pros
  • Flexible schedule and location
  • No commute saves 40+ min/day
  • Higher reported job satisfaction
  • Lower overhead costs for companies
  • Access to global talent pool
Cons
  • Social isolation and loneliness (33% report)
  • Blurred work-life boundaries
  • Harder to build team culture
  • Distractions at home

Office Work

Pros
  • Spontaneous collaboration & brainstorming
  • Stronger team bonding & culture
  • Clear work-life boundaries
  • Easier mentorship for juniors
  • Better access to equipment & resources
Cons
  • Long commutes (avg 27 min each way)
  • Higher overhead costs for companies
  • More frequent interruptions
  • Limited flexibility and autonomy

Employee Satisfaction

Key Insights

Hybrid Is the Future

83% of workers prefer a hybrid model — the best of both worlds, 2-3 days remote per week.

Productivity Gains

Remote workers complete 13% more tasks per day on average, with fewer distractions and meetings.

Mental Health

Remote workers report 25% less burnout, but 33% more loneliness — social connection matters.

Cost Savings

Companies save $11K per remote worker annually. Employees save $4K on commuting and meals.
